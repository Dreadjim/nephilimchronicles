What Is Forbidden Codex

Forbidden Codex is an evolving premium mythic archive built from scripture-shadow, apocrypha, ancient tradition, and dark reconstruction. It catalogs fallen angels, hybrid bloodlines, giant clans, abyssal entities, and pre-flood horrors through a living codex designed to expand across collections, volumes, and future worlds.

Primary Sources

Why This Matters

The Nephilim, the Watchers, Azazel, Samyaza, the Rephaim — these beings appear in texts that predate most of the Bible as we know it. The Book of Enoch, regarded as scripture by early Church fathers and found among the Dead Sea Scrolls, describes the 200 Watchers who descended on Mount Hermon, took wives among humanity, and sired the giant Nephilim bloodlines.

This is not a random lore dump and not generic dark fantasy. Forbidden Codex is built as a structured mythic system — a premium archive where scripture, apocrypha, giant bloodlines, abyssal creatures, and forbidden knowledge are catalogued as a living world.

Nephilim Chronicles is only the first volume. Forbidden Codex is designed to expand into future collections, creature lineages, giant dynasties, abyssal courts, and other dark mythic worlds yet to be revealed.

What This Is

A premium dark-mythic IP and living codex — documenting the Nephilim, Watchers, fallen angels, biblical giants, and pre-flood creatures from primary ancient sources including the Book of Enoch, Dead Sea Scrolls, Book of Jasher, and Genesis. Every entry is fully illustrated and cross-referenced against multiple ancient traditions. This is documented mythic history presented as a visual archive.

Every entry is sourced from real ancient texts — the Book of Enoch, the Talmud, the Dead Sea Scrolls, Ctesias's Persian records, the Iliad, and more. The Rephaim are referenced in Genesis, Isaiah, and Job. Azazel appears in Leviticus 16. The Manticore was documented by Greek and Persian historians in 400 BCE. These beings existed in the ancient imagination for thousands of years before any fantasy genre. This codex treats them with the weight and rigor they deserve.
