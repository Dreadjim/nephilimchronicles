The Manticore

The Silent Hunter · Persian Predator

CLASSIFICATION
Triple-Hybrid Beast
GENETIC BASE
Human + Lion + Scorpion
HEIGHT
4–5 ft shoulder / 12–15 ft total
STATUS
EXTINCT (c. 300 BCE)

Overview

DOMAIN
Predation, Mimicry, Venom
WEIGHT
500–700 lbs

The Head

Human-like male face with wide proportions, high cheekbones, and pronounced brow. Skin blends leathery and furred textures. Three rows of teeth: front for tearing, middle for grinding, rear for gripping—all continuously replaced. Amber eyes with vertical pupils. Voice mimics human and animal sounds with precision.

The Body

Crimson-furred lion frame, heavily muscled for sudden bursts. Larger than typical lions.

The Tail

Segmented, 4–6 feet long, flexible. Barbed stinger at tip. Launches spines up to 40 feet. Regenerates spines over weeks.

Venom

Rapid paralysis with intense pain; fatal within 30 minutes.

Hunting Approach

  1. Mimics familiar voices to draw attention.
  2. Fires spines silently from range.
  3. Closes with 50 mph bursts; climbs and leaps.
  4. Plans based on observed patterns.
  5. Consumes all traces of prey.

Active & Passive Abilities

VOICE MIMICAudible · No Save

Perfectly replicates human voices, animal calls, and ambient sound — accurate enough to fool those who know the original voice. Used exclusively to draw isolated targets away from groups before engagement.

ActivePsychological
SPINE VOLLEY40 ft · 3–12 spines

Launches barbed tail spines in silence. Three to twelve per volley. Each spine causes rapid progressive paralysis — three to five hits deliver a fatal dose within thirty minutes. Spines regenerate fully within days.

RangedVenom
TRIPLE-ROW CLAMPMelee · No Escape

Three continuous rows of teeth operate simultaneously — front for tearing, middle for grinding, rear for anchoring. Nothing bitten by the Manticore has been recorded pulling free. Continuous replacement means tooth loss is irrelevant.

MeleeRestraint
NO TRACEPost-Kill · Area

Consumes all physical evidence of its kills — bones, blood, equipment. Persian trackers followed one for six days and found only metallic fragments in the lair. This is why confirmation of the species was so difficult for ancient naturalists.

PassiveConcealment

⚠ Known Vulnerabilities & Counters

  • Large coordinated groups with ranged capability — the 300 BCE final record noted elephant deterrence at close range.
  • Aerial attack vectors: cannot effectively track threats from above while engaging ground targets.
  • Genetic instability: self-imposed extinction through near-zero reproductive capacity and progressive mental degradation.

Keeper's Notes

Ctesias's 400 BCE account is the earliest clinical description of the spine-launching mechanism — a detail so anatomically specific that later Greek compilers struggled to rationalise it as metaphor. The Manticore was documented by empire-level observers across three civilisations within a 200-year window. That is not myth diffusion. That is witness convergence.

Notable Encounters

Persian Hunt: Trackers followed one for six days; lair contained only metallic remnants.

Macedonian Reports: Scouts vanished after hearing comrades' voices at night.

Final Record: 300 BCE caravan in Zagros—spines paralyzed guards; elephants repelled the creature.

Why Extinction

Short lifespan, low reproduction, increasing mental decline led to extinction by 1200 BCE.

Theological Significance

The Manticore represents the corruption of natural order through forced hybridization—a predator designed to eliminate all traces of its prey, leaving no evidence of its existence.
