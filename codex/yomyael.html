Lore Record

Yomyael's name — 'Day of God' — names him as the custodian of divine time. In the ancient world, specific days carried specific significance: holy days, appointed times (mo'edim), days of divine judgment or favour. The one who could read the signs embedded in days had access to the divine schedule — and the ability to manipulate or counterfeit it. Yomyael's fall introduced the corruption of time itself: false holy days, misaligned calendars, and the substitution of human-devised timing for the divine appointed schedule.

The Yomyael Record

Sacred Calendar Corruption

The ancient Near East had elaborate sacred calendars: Babylonian, Egyptian, Hebrew. Each organised time around divine events. Yomyael's teaching introduced the ability to construct convincing false sacred calendars — holy days with no divine content.

Day-Signs Tradition

In Mesopotamian tradition, specific days were tracked as auspicious or inauspicious for various activities — a practice called hemerological divination. Yomyael sits at the origin of this tradition in the Nephilim Chronicles cosmology.

Yom in Hebrew Theology

Yom (day) in Hebrew carries more than temporal meaning. The 'Day of the Lord' (Yom YHWH) is an eschatological concept meaning the day of divine intervention and judgment. A being named Yom-El holds a title that directly references the end-time event he is waiting for.

Listed in 1 Enoch 6:7

Yomyael is listed among the twenty Watcher-chiefs who took the Hermon oath. His position confirms his role as a command-tier figure in the descent, not merely rank-and-file.

'These are the names of the holy angels who watch: Uriel... Raphael... Raguel... Michael... Saraqael... Gabriel... and Remiel.' The Watchers who fell held offices like these before their descent. — 1 Enoch 20 (listing the loyal Watchers)

The biblical tradition places enormous weight on time. The entire sacrificial system, the appointed feasts, the Sabbath — all are time-based covenants. When Yomyael taught the reading of day-signs as a craft divorced from divine authority, he introduced the possibility of a parallel calendar — a counterfeit temporal structure that looked sacred but was not divinely ordained. This is why so much of the prophetic tradition in the Hebrew Bible concerns false sacred calendars and misaligned festival observance: the corruption Yomyael introduced was not just theological but chronological. The pre-Flood world operated on a corrupted timetable. The Flood itself, in the calendar-obsessed Book of Jubilees, was a divine reset of time as well as creation. Yomyael's imprisonment is thus appropriate: the being who corrupted sacred time now waits, in timeless darkness, for the final Day — the one day he did not teach about, because he was not shown it.

Active & Passive Abilities

DAY-SIGN READINGPassive · Continuous

Yomyael reads the divine significance embedded in any specific day or moment. He perceives which times are divinely appointed for what purposes — and which appear to be appointed but are counterfeits. This makes him both a precise threat-assessor and an expert in temporal deception.

PassiveDivination
CALENDAR CORRUPTIONActive · Wide Range

Yomyael can introduce misalignment into the sacred calendar of a community — shifting the perceived holy days, disrupting fasting cycles, replacing divinely-timed events with counterfeit sacred moments that carry no actual covenant content.

ActiveMass Corruption
FALSE APPOINTED TIMEActive · Targeted

Yomyael can make a specific day feel sacred when it is not — filling it with the phenomenological texture of divine presence (peace, solemnity, light) without the actual divine content. Communities built their entire covenant rhythms around such moments. False ones are indistinguishable.

ActiveDeception

⚠ Known Vulnerabilities & Counters

  • The true sacred calendar — Yomyael's false timing cannot survive comparison with genuinely divinely-appointed times. Communities with intact covenant chronology are resistant to his calendar corruption.
  • Specific divine name invocations tied to time — the Shema recited at the commanded times, the Sabbath properly observed — create temporal anchors he cannot dislodge.
  • The Flood reset — the event that recalibrated divine time fundamentally weakened his influence. His corrupted calendar was wiped. He has had to rebuild it since.

Keeper's Notes

Yomyael is the Watcher for campaign designers who want to work with time as a mechanic. His presence warps the in-game calendar — the party finds themselves arriving at a festival that shouldn't be happening, or missing a divinely-appointed event because the timing felt wrong. He is the Watcher behind every community that worshipped with sincerity on the wrong day, not knowing they had been given a counterfeit sacred time.
